# Instagram Reels Playbook (9:16)

**Internal team playbook — updated July 2026**

This is our working standard for every Reel we produce. Follow it unless a test explicitly says otherwise.

---

## 1. Technical Specs

| Spec | Standard |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical |
| Resolution | 1080 × 1920 px minimum |
| Format / codec | MP4, H.264 |
| Frame rate | 30 fps (60 fps for fast motion) |
| Max length for recommendations | 3 minutes (longer = ineligible for non-follower reach) |
| Cover | Design at 1080 × 1920; check the 1:1 grid crop (center) |

### Safe zones (on a 1080 × 1920 canvas)

- **Top:** keep text/logos out of the top ~220 px (camera icon, dynamic islands)
- **Bottom:** keep out of the bottom ~420–450 px (caption, audio attribution, CTA overlays)
- **Right:** keep ~120 px clear (like/comment/share/save button stack)
- **Left:** keep ~60 px margin
- **Hook text sweet spot:** centered horizontally, roughly 200–600 px from the top — safe on every device
- Never place text in the bottom 15% of the frame

---

## 2. How the Algorithm Ranks Reels (2026)

Instagram runs separate ranking systems per surface. Reels is the discovery engine — most views come from **non-followers**. The three signals Adam Mosseri has confirmed matter most:

1. **Watch time** — the #1 signal. Total seconds watched, % completed, rewatches.
2. **Sends per reach** — DM shares are weighted roughly **3–5× higher than likes** for reaching new audiences. Before posting, ask: "Would someone send this to a friend?"
3. **Likes per reach** — ratio, not raw count. 50 likes on 1,000 views beats 100 likes on 10,000.

Also weighted: **saves** (~3× a like, signals lasting value) and **completion rate** (above 50% = strong distribution signal; instant replays are gold).

### Critical thresholds

- **First 3 seconds:** ~50% of viewers drop off here. If 60%+ watch past 3 seconds, Instagram pushes wider.
- **First 30–60 minutes:** engagement velocity in this window decides non-follower distribution. Reply to every early comment; share to Story immediately after posting.
- Reels with a 3-second hold above 60% out-reach weak holds by 5–10×.

### Eligibility — instant disqualifiers

- Watermarks from other platforms (TikTok, CapCut logos)
- Unlicensed audio
- Recycled/reposted content — Instagram's "Originality Score" detects re-uploads, even lightly edited ones. **10+ reposts in 30 days = excluded from all recommendations.** Original content earns 40–60% more reach.
- Over 3 minutes

---

## 3. The Hook (First 1–3 Seconds)

The hook decides everything. Script it word-for-word before filming, and shoot multiple takes.

**Rules:**

- Hook text on screen within the first 1.5 seconds
- Visual movement or change in the first second — never a static open, never a logo animation
- No intros. "Hey guys, welcome back" is a death sentence.
- Face in frame boosts retention ~35%
- Test the hook **on mute** — if it doesn't grab silently, revise

**Proven hook formulas:**

| Formula | Example | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| Contrarian | "Stop using hashtags. Here's what to do instead." | Myth-busting |
| Mistake | "You're making this error with X" | Educational |
| Outcome | "This got us 745 followers in 30 days — no ads" | Results/proof |
| Audience-specific | "If you're a [role] struggling with [problem]…" | Niche targeting |
| Show the end first | Reveal the "after," then rewind to the how | Tutorials, transformations |
| Question | "Would you pay $500 for this if it saved 10 hours a week?" | Product/service |
| Pain point | "Sick of posting daily and seeing nothing?" | Problem–solution |
| DM-integrated | "Comment X and I'll DM you Y" | Lead gen |

---

## 4. Length & Pacing

Use the **shortest length that delivers the message**. A 10-second Reel at 80% retention beats a 60-second Reel at 30% every time.

| Content type | Length |
|---|---|
| Trending sounds / viral formats | 7–15 s |
| Quick tips, showcases | 15–30 s |
| Tutorials, educational | 30–60 s |
| Storytelling, behind-the-scenes | 60–90 s |
| In-depth guides (established audience only) | 90–180 s |

**Editing rules:** cut every 1.5–3 seconds; jump-cut out all dead air and pauses; burned-in captions/subtitles (≈38% longer retention; most viewers watch muted — max two lines per beat); end with a payoff or a seamless loop.

**Retention benchmarks (view-through rate):** under 15 s — good >65%, exceptional >80%. 15–30 s — good >50%, strong >60%.

---

## 5. Audio Strategy

Target mix: **40–50% original audio/voiceover** (algorithmic preference in 2026 + brand voice), **30–40% trending sounds**, **15–20% evergreen/mood music**.

Finding trending audio: the Trending tab in the Reels music picker; the upward-arrow icon next to audio while scrolling; Professional Dashboard → Trending Audio. Target sounds with **under 5K uses** — trends migrate from TikTok in 1–2 weeks, so watch there for early signals. Business accounts: verify audio licensing; use Meta's royalty-free library for anything promotional.

---

## 6. Captions, Keywords & Hashtags

- Instagram search is keyword-driven now. Put primary keywords in the **caption first line**, on-screen text, and spoken audio.
- Hashtags are filing labels, not growth levers: **3–5 specific, relevant tags** max. Hashtag-stuffing correlates with *lower* reach.
- Ask specific questions in captions — "What's the biggest mistake you've made with X?" beats "Thoughts?" The algorithm now weights **conversation depth**: long comments, threads, and fast creator replies count far more than emoji comments.

---

## 7. Publishing & Cadence

- **Cadence: 3–5 Reels/week.** Quality and watch time beat volume; 10+/week gives diminishing returns and burnout.
- Post during audience peak windows (check Insights → Most Active Times). General benchmarks: 7–9 AM, 11 AM–1 PM, 5–7 PM local.
- Immediately after posting: share to Story, reply to every comment in the first 30–60 minutes.
- Suggested format mix per week: 3–4 Reels, 2–3 carousels, 1–2 static posts.
- Content pillar mix: **40% value** (tips/education), **25% relatable** (humor, trends, pain points), **20% behind-the-scenes**, **15% promotional**.

---

## 8. Trial Reels — Test Before You Commit

Trial Reels publish to **non-followers only**; followers never see a flop, so your account's engagement averages are protected.

Use them to: test new hooks on the same content, try new formats/topics, experiment with longer lengths, and A/B test before committing. If a trial performs, share it to followers.

**Note:** trials lack the follower engagement boost — only compare trial results against other trials.

---

## 9. What Kills Reach

- Reposting others' content or cross-posting watermarked TikToks
- Slow build-up openings, logo intros, greetings
- Banned/flagged hashtags, spammy engagement tactics, unofficial third-party tools
- Low-res exports, text buried under UI elements
- Check **Settings → Account Status** monthly for recommendation limits; if flagged, stop the behavior and expect 7–14 days to recover.

---

## 10. Pre-Publish Checklist

**Before filming:** pillar chosen · hook scripted word-for-word · audio selected · 9:16 confirmed · lighting/audio set

**Filming:** movement in first 1.5 s · face in frame · cuts every 1.5–3 s · multiple hook takes

**Post:** burned-in captions · no watermarks · 1080×1920 export · text inside safe zones · loop or payoff ending

**Publish:** keywords in caption · 3–5 hashtags · peak-time slot · share to Story · reply to comments within the first hour

---

## Metrics We Track (monthly)

- 3-second hold rate (target 60%+)
- View-through/completion rate vs. benchmarks in §4
- Sends per reach (our #1 growth metric)
- Saves per reach
- Follower vs. non-follower view split (healthy discovery ≈ 55%+ non-followers)
- Engagement rate vs. account-size benchmark (<5K followers ≈ 3.8%, 5–50K ≈ 2.1%, 50–200K ≈ 1.4%)
